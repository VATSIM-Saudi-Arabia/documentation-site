## 1. Overview
**Aerodrome Flight Information Service (AFIS)** is an aerodrome-based air traffic service provided at aerodromes where the establishment of a full aerodrome control service is not justified. AFIS units exist to support the safe and efficient conduct of aerodrome traffic by providing flight information and alerting service to aircraft operating in the aerodrome environment.

---

## 2. Purpose
The main purpose of AFIS is to improve safety and efficiency at aerodromes with traffic levels that do not require a control tower. By giving pilots timely and accurate information, the AFIS unit helps them manage arrivals, departures, taxiing, and traffic pattern operations with better situational awareness.

AFIS also contributes to alerting service, meaning it supports the notification and response process in abnormal or emergency situations. This makes AFIS an important part of the aerodrome’s overall safety framework.

### 2.1. Scope
AFIS is commonly established at aerodromes where:

- Traffic levels are relatively low or moderate;
- Traffic complexity does not justify a control tower;
- Operational risk can be managed through advisory information; and
- Full ATC services are not economically or operationally necessary

!!! note "Beware!"
    AFIS is **NOT** an air traffic control service. AFIS personnel do not issue takeoff clearances, landing clearances, taxi clearances, or separation instructions. The service is purely informational in nature.

### 2.2 Key Responsibility
Because AFIS is not a control service, it is the **responsibility of pilots** using the service to maintain proper separation in conformity with the rules of the air. When operating near an aerodrome where AFIS is provided, pilots must;

- Use information received from the AFIS unit;
- Combine it with their own knowledge and observations;
- Decide on the course of action to ensure separation from other aircraft, ground vehicles, and obstacles.

!!! info
    In addition to flight information service, AFIS units provide alerting service to support search and rescue notification and emergency coordination. Such situations involving **Aircraft emergencies**, **Radio communication failure**, **Overdue aircraft**, **Accidents or incidents** and **Distress or urgency conditions**.

## 3. Communication Requirements and Procedures

### 3.1. Radio Communication Capability
Aircraft should, unless exempted by the appropriate ATS authority, be capable of two-way radio communications with the AFIS unit on the prescribed frequency. For AFIS to function effectively, pilots must:

- Establish and maintain two-way radio communication with the AFIS unit;
- Report their positions to the AFIS unit;
- Report their levels (altitudes);
- Report all significant manoeuvres, and;
- Report their intentions

The efficiency of the AFIS is dependent upon the information received from pilots. Without accurate and timely pilot reports, the AFIS officer cannot provide effective service.

### 3.2. Communication Timing Standards
For arriving and departing aircraft in the vicinity of an aerodrome where AFIS is established:

| **Aircraft Status** |   **When to Establish Communication**   |    **When to Continue Communication**   |
|:--------------------|:----------------------------------------|:----------------------------------------|
|       Arrival       | Generally within 20 NM of the aerodrome | No later than 10 NM prior to the ATZ    |
|      Departure      | Before departure                        | Until not less than 5 NM beyond the ATZ |

Throughout the operational periods, the AFIS controller must:

- Monitor the frequency continuously during operational hours;
- Acknowledge all pilot communications promptly;
- Ensure pilots understand they must report positions, levels, manoeuvres, and intentions and;
- Remind pilots of communication requirements if they lapse

## 4. Provision of Basic Information
AFIS units provide the following **basic information** to aircraft. Controllers must be proficient in delivering each type accurately and promptly.


| **Information Type**                       | **Details Provided**                                                                                             | **When Applicable**                                                        |
|:-------------------------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|:---------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Meteorological Information**             | SIGMET/AIRMET information                                                                                        | For aircraft about to take off or land                                     |
| **Surface Wind**                           | Current direction and speed, including significant variations; indicates "preferred runway"                      | For all take-off and landing operations                                    |
| **QNH Altimeter Setting**                  | Current QNH value                                                                                                | For all aircraft                                                           |
| **Air Temperature**                        | Temperature reading                                                                                              | When requested for turbine engine aircraft take-off                        |
| **Visibility**                             | Current visibility in direction of take-off/initial climb OR approach/landing area (if <10 KM); RVR if available | For take-off, landing, and when visibility <10 KM                          |
| **Significant Meteorological Conditions**  | Weather conditions in take-off/climb-out area OR approach/landing area                                           | When significant conditions exist in these areas                           |
| **Present Weather & Low Cloud**            | Current weather; amount and height of base of low cloud                                                          | For aircraft making approach in IMC (Instrument Meteorological Conditions) |
| **Traffic & Hazard Information**           | Known aircraft, vehicles, or personnel on or near maneuvering area that may constitute hazard                    | When present and potentially hazardous                                     |
| **Aerodrome Conditions & Navigation Aids** | Aerodrome conditions; status of navigation aids; messages including clearances from ATS units for relay          | Whenever conditions or aids status change or are requested                 |
| **Other Safety Information**               | Any other information contributing to safety                                                                     | As needed for safety                                                       |


### 4.1. Meteorological Information for Take-off or Landing
Provide meteorological information for aircraft about to take off or land, including:

- SIGMET (Significant Meteorological Information)
- AIRMET (Airmen's Meteorological Information)
- These advisories warn of weather phenomena that may affect flight safety

### 4.2. Current Surface Wind Information
Provide the current surface wind direction and speed, including:

- Significant variations in wind;
- Indication of the "preferred runway";
- Current wind direction and speed;
- Other relevant aircraft in or about to enter the traffic pattern

### 4.3. QNH Altimeter Setting
Provide the current QNH altimeter setting, which allows pilots to set their altimeters to indicate elevation above mean sea level.

### 4.4. Air Temperature (on Request)
Provide air temperature when requested, specifically in the case of take-off by turbine engine aircraft. Turbine aircraft performance calculations require accurate temperature data.

### 4.5. Visibility Information
Provide the current visibility in:

- The direction of take-off and initial climb, OR;
- The approach and landing area if less than 10 KM;
- RVR (Runway Visual Range) if available

!!! info
    For aircraft making an approach in **IMC (Instrument Meteorological Conditions)**, The following information is critical for instrument approach procedures and must provide:

    - The present weather;
    - The amount of low cloud;
    - The height of the base of low cloud

### 4.6. Significant Meteorological Conditions
Report significant meteorological conditions in:

- The take-off and climb-out area, OR;
- The approach and landing area

### 4.7. Traffic and Hazard Information
Provide information on:

- Other known aircraft on or near the maneuvering area;
- Vehicles on or near the maneuvering area;
- Personnel on or near the maneuvering area;
- Any that may constitute a hazard

### 4.8. Aerodrome and Navigation Aid Status

- Aerodrome conditions (e.g., runway status, closures, temporary obstacles);
- Status of navigation aids (e.g., VOR, ILS, NDB operational status);
- Messages, including clearances received from ATS units for relay to aircraft

## 5. Phraseology
AFIS is responsible for recieving the required information for the concerned aircraft. Pilots may be expected to report the following information on first contact:

- Callsign;
- Aircraft type;
- Registration (Optional);
- Runway in use;
- Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA) or; Estimated Time of Departure (ETD);
- Intentions

### 5.1. Departures
Pushback, taxi, and takeoff operations are under the pilot discretion and the concerned traffic shall be updated with basic information. When transmitting, the **standard ICAO phrasesology** is used. For departing traffic, the following phrasesolgy must strictly be adhered to.

#### 5.1.1. IFR Clearance

> **Pilot**: _"Al-Ula Information, SVA1228, Aircraft type A320, HZ-ASB, departing runway 33R at ETD 1530z, requesting IFR to Riyadh"_

> **AFIS**: _"SVA1228, Al-Ula Information, cleared to Riyadh as filed, Runway 30 in use, Fly runway heading, Initial climb 4000ft, SQWK XXXX"_

> **AFIS**: _"SVA1228, Readback Correct, Information A, report ready for push and start"_

#### 5.1.2. Pushback & Startup Procedures

>  **Pilot**: _"SVA1228, Stand S15, ready for pushback"_

> **AFIS**: _"SVA1228, Push & start at your own discretion, no traffic reported on the apron, report ready for taxi"_

#### 5.1.3. Taxi Procedures

>  **Pilot**: _"SVA1228, Stand S15, ready for taxi"_

> **AFIS**: _"SVA1228, roger, no traffic reported on the apron and runway, report ready for departure"_

## 2.7.1 Radar Identification
Vectors may be assigned to confirm the identity of an aircraft. Other methods for ATS surveillance service identification consist of:

- **Utilizing position report information**

- **Requesting the aircraft to perform turns**

- **Using bearing and distance** (information from a prominent object or radio aid)

- **Transfer of control**

- **Use of SSR**

|                      **Controller Transmissions**                        |                                   **Pilot Transmissions**                                  |
|:------------------------------------------------------------------------:|:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------:|
|             **"SAUDIA 123 FOR IDENTIFICATION TURN LEFT BY 030 DEGREES"**              |                                                                                            |
|                                                                          |                     **"LEFT BY 030 DEGREES FOR IDENTIFICATION, SAUDIA 123"**                     |

In case identification may be lost, or about to be lost, the pilot should be warned and given appropriate instructions

|                      **Controller Transmissions**                        |                                   **Pilot Transmissions**                                  |
|:------------------------------------------------------------------------:|:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------:|
|             **"SAUDIA 123, RADAR CONTACT LOST, CONTACT JEDDAH CONTROL 126.500"**              |                                                                                            |
|                                                                          |                     **"JEDDAH CONTROL 126.500, SAUDIA 123"**                     |


## 2.7.2 Traffic Information
Details concerning traffic on a converging course should be provided in this format:

- **Relative bearing of the potential conflict using the 12-hour clock**

- **Distance from the conflicting traffic**

- **Direction of the other flight**

- Any additional pertinent information, for example:

- **Opposite (or same) direction**

- **Crossing from left to right (or right to left)**

If available, the aircraft type and level, including if it is climbing or descending

Avoiding action must be issued by the controller if an imminent risk of collision is likely without an immediate maneuver.


## 2.7.3 IFR Departures 
A majority of airports manage both arrivals and departures using a solitary control unit. At higher-traffic locations, departures and arrivals are managed by dedicated departure and arrival controllers.
Beyond the ATC route clearance, extra instructions for separation purposes might be provided either before or following take-off.

## 2.7.4 IFR Arrivals
Upon **initial contact**, approach control must inform the aircraft of the **expected approach type** (such as ILS, RNAV, or Visual).

|                                        **Controller Transmissions**                                        |                                            **Pilot Transmissions**                                          |
|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------:|:-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------:|
|           **"SAUDIA 123, IDENTIFIED,EXPECT ILS Y 34R, JEDDAH INFORMATION D"**          |                                                                                                             |
|                                                                                                            |         **"EXPECT ILS Y 34R, JEDDAH INFORMATION D, SAUDIA 123"**      |
|                                 

Occasionally, IFR aircraft might not finish the ILS approach procedure, requesting instead to execute a **visual approach**. Once the criteria for a visual approach are satisfied, the pilot can initiate the request by stating **“REQUEST VISUAL APPROACH”**. Controllers will approve this when traffic conditions allow.

Upon receiving clearance from ATC for a visual approach, subsequent descent is unrestricted, unless a particular limitation is specified within the clearance itself or provided in a later instruction.

## 2.7.5 Clearance via SID
For aircraft on a SID, clearances regarding remaining published level or speed restrictions must clearly state if those restrictions still apply or are cancelled. The standard phraseologies and their definitions are as follows:

**CLIMB VIA SID TO**:

- Ascend to the cleared level while obeying all published level restrictions.
- Adhere to the lateral profile of the SID.
- Observe published speed restrictions or any speed control instructions issued by ATC.

**CLIMB VIA SID TO, CANCEL LEVEL RESTRICTION(S)**:

- Ascend to the cleared level; all published level restrictions are void.
- Adhere to the lateral profile of the SID.
- Observe published speed restrictions or any speed control instructions issued by ATC.

**CLIMB VIA SID TO, CANCEL LEVEL RESTRICTION(S) AT**:

- Ascend to the cleared level; published level restrictions at the named points are void.
- Adhere to the lateral profile of the SID.
- Observe published speed restrictions or any speed control instructions issued by ATC.

**CLIMB VIA SID TO, CANCEL SPEED RESTRICTION(S)**:

- Ascend to the cleared level while obeying all published level restrictions.
- Adhere to the lateral profile of the SID.
- All published and ATC-issued speed restrictions are void.

**CLIMB UNRESTRICTED TO**, or **CLIMB TO, CANCEL LEVEL AND SPEED RESTRICTION(S)**:

- Ascend to the cleared level; all published level restrictions are void.
- Adhere to the lateral profile of the SID.
- All published and ATC-issued speed restrictions are void.

If no published level or speed restrictions remain on the SID, the instruction CLIMB TO is used. If the cleared level remains the same but new speed instructions are given, the phrase CLIMB VIA SID TO is left out.

When a departing flight is cleared **direct** to a waypoint on the SID, restrictions for bypassed points are cancelled, but all subsequent restrictions remain in effect. If an aircraft is vectored or cleared to a point off the SID, all SID speed and level restrictions are cancelled, and the controller must:

Restate the cleared level.

Issue new speed or level restrictions as required.

Inform the pilot if the aircraft is expected to re-join the SID later.

**ATC instructions for an aircraft to re-join a SID must consist of:**

- The SID designator to be re-joined, unless prior notification of the re-join was already given as described above.
- The cleared level for the re-joining phase of the SID, following the previously mentioned procedures.
- The specific location where the aircraft is anticipated to re-enter the SID.

### 2.7.6 Clearance via STAR
For aircraft on a STAR, clearances regarding any remaining published level or speed restrictions must specify whether those rules still apply or are being cancelled. The standard phraseologies and their meanings are as follows:

**DESCEND VIA STAR TO:**

- Lower the altitude to the cleared level while adhering to all published level restrictions.
- Follow the lateral profile of the STAR.
- Comply with published speed restrictions or any speed control instructions issued by ATC.

**DESCEND VIA STAR TO, CANCEL LEVEL RESTRICTION(S):**

- Lower the altitude to the cleared level; all published level restrictions are voided.
- Follow the lateral profile of the STAR.
- Comply with published speed restrictions or any speed control instructions issued by ATC.
- DESCEND VIA STAR TO, CANCEL LEVEL RESTRICTION(S) AT:
- Lower the altitude to the cleared level; level restrictions at the named points are voided.
- Follow the lateral profile of the STAR.
- Comply with published speed restrictions or any speed control instructions issued by ATC.

**DESCEND VIA STAR TO, CANCEL SPEED RESTRICTION(S):**

- Lower the altitude to the cleared level while adhering to all published level restrictions.
- Follow the lateral profile of the STAR.
- All published and ATC-issued speed restrictions are voided.

**DESCEND VIA STAR TO, CANCEL SPEED RESTRICTION(S) AT:**

- Lower the altitude to the cleared level while adhering to all published level restrictions.
- Follow the lateral profile of the STAR.
- Published speed restrictions at the specified locations are voided.

**DESCEND UNRESTRICTED TO** or **DESCEND TO, CANCEL LEVEL AND SPEED RESTRICTION(S):**

- Lower the altitude to the cleared level; all published level restrictions are voided.
- Follow the lateral profile of the STAR.
- All published and ATC-issued speed restrictions are voided.

If no published level or speed restrictions remain on the STAR, the phrase DESCEND TO is used. If the cleared level is the same but new speed instructions are issued, the phrase DESCEND VIA STAR TO is omitted.

When an arriving flight is cleared direct to a waypoint on the STAR, any **restrictions for the bypassed waypoints are cancelled**, though all subsequent restrictions remain in effect. If an aircraft is vectored or cleared to a point off the STAR, **all published restrictions for that STAR are cancelled**, and the controller must:

- Restate the cleared level.
- Issue new speed or level restrictions as required.
- Notify the pilot if the aircraft is expected to re-join the STAR at a later point.

**ATC instructions for an aircraft to re-enter a STAR must include:**

- The specific STAR designator to be re-joined, except when prior notice of the re-join has already been given as described previously.

- The cleared level for the re-joining segment of the STAR, following the procedures mentioned above.

- The exact location where the aircraft is expected to re-join the STAR profile.

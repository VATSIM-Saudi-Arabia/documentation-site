## Designated Area of Responsibility

"_Jeddah Final West_" (OEJN_FW_APP) is responsible for handling all arrivals from the downwind leg landing on runway 16R/34L.

## Notes

- The separation minima within the Jeddah TMA is **5 nautical miles**. If this cannot be achieved, a minimum vertical separation of **1,000 feet** must be maintained.
  - On final approach, the lateral separation can be reduced to **3 NM**.
- Wake Turbulence Separation must be enforced under 6,000 feet.
- After handoff from Jeddah Approach [APP 1], all aircraft should be descended to **even** altitudes. Below are the recommended altitudes:
  - 8,000 ft, 6,000 ft, 4,000 ft, 3,000 ft (establishing altitude).
- Independent Parallel Approaches (Mode 1) will be in use for Hajj OPS: 2026.
  - RWY 34L is the **low side**, with an establishing altitude of 3,000 ft.
  - Aircraft **must be level** at 3,000 ft prior to establishing with the localiser, otherwise this will be **deemed a conflict**.
- The standard vector headings are below:
  - **Base Leg -** 070
  - **Intercepting Vector -** 010 (340 - 010)
- Before turning base, aircraft must of **vacated 4,000 ft**. Aircraft should not be turned onto base prior to passing 3,7000 ft to avoid a conflict.

- When established, aircraft should be kept:
  - "180 knots maintain to 10 DME, 160 knots maintain to 4 DME."
- "SVA123, contact Jeddah Tower, callsign only, 118.200."

## Handoffs from Jeddah Approach [APP 1]

- The tables below dictate the altitudes and fixes at which arrival traffic will be handed off to from Jedday Approach [APP 1]. These are to be **strictly adhered to**.
- A minimum longitudinal spacing of **7 NM** shall be targeted by APP 1.
- The target speed for handoffs from APP 1 is **210 knots**, with the maximum being **220 knots**. This will be indicated in the tag.
